Deployment Strategies Compared

Choosing the right deployment strategy balances risk, speed, and rollback complexity.

🔄

Rolling Update

Gradually replace old instances with new ones, a few at a time. Zero downtime but harder to rollback. Kubernetes default strategy.

  • Pods replaced batch-by-batch
  • Load balancer routes to healthy pods only
  • Max surge & max unavailable configurable
Risk: Low · Speed: Medium
🔵🟢

Blue-Green Deployment

Maintain two identical environments. Switch traffic from Blue (live) to Green (new) instantly. Instant rollback by switching back.

  • Two identical production environments
  • DNS/load-balancer flip to switch
  • Instant rollback — flip back
Risk: Very Low · Cost: 2× infra
🐤

Canary Release

Route a small percentage of traffic (e.g. 5%) to the new version. Monitor metrics. Gradually increase or rollback based on health signals.

  • 5% → 25% → 50% → 100% traffic shift
  • Real user testing with minimal blast radius
  • Automated promotion/rollback via Argo Rollouts
Risk: Lowest · Best for: high-traffic
🚩

Feature Flags

Deploy code to production but control who sees features via runtime configuration. Decouple deployment from release — ship continuously without exposing unfinished work.

  • Toggle features per user, cohort, or region
  • A/B testing and staged rollouts
  • Kill-switch for instant disable
Flexibility: Max · LaunchDarkly, Flagsmith

Measuring Your Pipeline Performance

The four DORA metrics are the industry-standard way to measure DevOps effectiveness.

⚡

Deployment Frequency

On-Demand

Elite: Multiple per day. How often your team deploys to production.

⏱️

Lead Time for Changes

< 1 Hour

Elite: < 1 hour. Commit to production deployment time.

🔧

Time to Restore

< 1 Hour

Elite: < 1 hour. How fast you recover from a production incident.

🛡️

Change Failure Rate

0–5%

Elite: 0–5%. Percentage of deployments causing production failures.
