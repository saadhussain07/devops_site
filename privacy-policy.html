1. Overview

DevOps Hub ("we," "us," "our," or "Company") operates the website at https://rocketdevops.vercel.app (the "Site"). We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring you have a positive experience on our Site.

This Privacy Policy applies to information we collect through the Site and explains our privacy practices.

2. Information We Collect

Automatically Collected Information

When you visit our Site, we automatically collect certain information about your device and how you interact with the Site, including:

  • Log Data: IP address, browser type, operating system, pages visited, time spent on pages, and referral sources
  • Device Information: Device type, operating system, unique device identifiers, and mobile network information
  • Usage Data: Clicks, scrolls, searches, and interactions with content

Information You Provide

We collect information you voluntarily provide when you:

  • Fill out our contact form (name, email, message)
  • Subscribe to updates or newsletters (if available)
  • Leave comments or feedback
  • Participate in surveys or quizzes

Cookies & Similar Technologies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience. See the Cookies & Tracking section for details.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use collected information for the following purposes:

  • To Operate the Site: Maintain, improve, and optimize Site functionality and performance
  • To Respond to Inquiries: Answer questions submitted through the contact form
  • To Understand Usage: Analyze how users interact with our content to improve it
  • To Send Communications: Send newsletters, updates, or marketing content (with your consent)
  • Legal Compliance: Comply with applicable laws, regulations, and legal requests
  • Fraud Prevention: Detect, prevent, and address fraud and security issues

4. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help remember your preferences and improve your experience.

Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential Cookies: Required for basic Site functionality
  • Performance Cookies: Help us understand how you use the Site (via analytics)
  • Preference Cookies: Remember your choices (e.g., dark/light mode)

Analytics

We may use analytics tools (such as Google Analytics or similar services) to understand user behavior and improve our Site. These tools may collect information about your visits, including pages viewed and time spent on the Site.

Controlling Cookies

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to refuse cookies or alert you when cookies are being sent. However, disabling cookies may affect Site functionality.

Our contact form uses Formspree (a third-party service) to process submissions. Formspree may collect and process your name, email, and message. Please review Formspree's privacy policy for details: https://formspree.io/legal/privacy-policy/

Analytics & Monitoring

If we implement analytics services (e.g., Plausible Analytics, Umami, Google Analytics), we will update this policy. These services may collect anonymized usage data.

External Links

Our Site may contain links to third-party websites. We are not responsible for their privacy practices. Please review their privacy policies before providing personal information.

6. Data Retention

  • Contact Form Data: Retained for customer service purposes, typically 1-2 years unless you request deletion
  • Analytics Data: Typically retained for 12-24 months
  • Cookies: Session cookies are deleted when you close your browser; persistent cookies expire based on their settings

You may request deletion of your data at any time by contacting us (see Contact section).

7. Your Rights & Choices

GDPR Rights (EU/UK Users)

If you are in the EU or UK, you have the following rights under GDPR:

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of your personal data
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your data ("Right to be Forgotten")
  • Right to Restrict Processing: Limit how we use your data
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a portable format
  • Right to Object: Object to data processing

CCPA Rights (California Users)

If you are a California resident, you have the right to:

  • Know what personal data is collected
  • Delete personal data (with some exceptions)
  • Opt-out of the sale of personal data

How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at the email address provided in the Contact Us section.

8. Data Security

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. However, no method of transmission over the Internet is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

If you believe your data has been compromised, please contact us immediately.

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or applicable laws. We will notify you of material changes by updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of the Site after changes indicates your acceptance of the updated policy.
